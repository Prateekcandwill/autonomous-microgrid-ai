Abstract

Rural electrification and climate resilience are critical for sustainable development. This project presents an AI-powered microgrid integrating multi-agent reinforcement learning, physics-informed neural networks, and blockchain energy governance for smart villages. Our approach demonstrates 95%+ renewables, 99.9% uptime, energy democracy, and social empowerment—transforming rural energy access and climate adaptation.

Project Methodology

Data Collection
Comprehensive gathering of climate, consumption, and community data to feed AI forecasting and optimization models.
AI Model Training
Development and disciplined tuning of Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning (MARL) and Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINN) for grid and weather prediction.
Blockchain Setup
Implementation of smart contracts for peer-to-peer energy trading, carbon credits, and local governance automation.
Deployment & Testing
Field trials and simulation testing for resilience, reliability, economic performance, and community feedback.

Four-Layer Intelligent Framework

Physical Layer
Renewable generation (solar, wind), battery storage, smart meters, and EV charging infrastructure—enabling digital sensing and local energy autonomy.
AI Control Layer
Multi-agent RL agents operate each asset; PINNs forecast weather and resource trends; edge computing ensures real-time distributed intelligence.
Blockchain Layer
Automated smart contracts validate peer-to-peer trading, manage community participation, and maintain transparent carbon accounting.
Governance Layer
Community dashboards, voting systems, data visualization, and policy management for local engagement and democratic decision-making.
